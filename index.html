A Tribute to Professor Stephen Hawking
(1942 - 2018)

Cosmologist, space traveller and hero.

Famed physicist Stephen Hawking.

A Brief History of Time - Professor Stephen Hawking

  • 1942 - Born on 8th January 1942 (exactly 300 years after the death of Galileo) in Oxford, England.
  • 1952 - Stephen went to St. Albans School and then on to University College, Oxford; his father's old college.
  • 1962 - In October, Stephen arrived at the Department of Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics (DAMTP) at the University of Cambridge to do research in cosmology.
  • 1963 - Stephen was diagnosed with ALS, a form of Motor Neurone Disease, shortly after his 21st birthday.
  • 1965 - Gained a PhD with his thesis titled 'Properties of Expanding Universes' and bcame a research fellow.
  • 1966 - He won the Adams Prize for his essay 'Singularities and the Geometry of Space-time'.
  • 1968 - Stephen moved to the Institute of Astronomy.
  • 1969 - He bacame a Fellow for Distinction in Science at Gonville & Caius college.
  • 1970 - With Roger Penrose he showed that Einstein's general theory of relativity implied space and time would have a beginning in the Big Bang and an end in black holes.
  • 1973 - Moved back to DAMTP where he published his first academic book with George Ellis, The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time.
  • 1974 - Stephen was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society.
  • 1975 - He became a Reader in Gravitational Physics at DAMTP.
  • 1977 - He progressed to Professor of Gravitational Physics.
  • 1979 - 2009 - He held the position of Lucasian Professor of Mathematics.
  • 1982 - Awarded a CBE.
  • 1988 - Awarded Wolf Foundation prize.
  • 1989 - Awarded the Companion of Honour.
  • 2006 - Awarded Copley Medal.
  • 2009 - Awarded Presidential Medal of Freedom.
  • 2013 - Awarded Fundamental Physics prize.
  • 2015 - Hawking received the BBVA Foundation Frontiers of Knowledge Award in Basic Sciences shared with Viatcheslav Mukhanov for discovering that the galaxies were formed from quantum fluctuations in the early Universe.
  • 2018 - In March 2018, it was announced that two Russian astronomers who discovered GRB180316A, a newborn black hole in the Ophiuchus constellation on 16 March 2018, had dedicated their find to Stephen Hawking, having discovered it two days after his death.
